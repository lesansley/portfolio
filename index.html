Triathlon

Triathlons are performed over a variety of distances. The most common (in ascending order of distance (km) are sprint (0.75/20/5), Olympic (1.5/40/10), long course (2.5/80/20) and Ironman (3.8/180/42) triathlons.
Swimming, cycling and running contribute roughly 15%, 55% and 30%, respectively, to the total duration of an Olympic distance triathlon.

Swim

Swimming is the first leg of a standard triathlon and is performed over distances between 750 m and 3.8 km. Wetsuits are permitted in open water swims when the water temperature is below 22°C.

Bike

Stefan Holm / Shutterstock.com
The bike leg follows the transition from the water. For age-group athletes drafting on the bike is illegal in triathlons over all distances. However elite competitors are permitted to draft during the shorter triathlons up to and including Olympic distance.

Run

The run is the last leg of a triathlon and usually decides the race. In Ironman™ triathlons the run is competed over a full marathon distance of 42 km.
